Zyahna Bryant’s Parents and Background

On November 24,  2001, Zyahna Bryant (age 22) was born in Charlottesville, Virginia, America. Raised by her single mother, Zeneida Howard, she shares her upbringing with two siblings. A spotlight marked Zyahna’s early years on her struggle with weight.

In 2008, her mother highlighted the challenges they faced in addressing her weight issues through a news article. The public eye continued to follow her journey. By the time she was 12, Zyahna had organized her first rally advocating for justice in the tragic case of Trayvon Martin, a victim of police violence.

Bryant Became the Face of BLM Protests

In 2015, Zyahna made her mark as a panelist, joining local organizers and Bernie Sanders at a Town Hall in Charlottesville. The discussion revolved around the importance of funding public education and grants.

She further solidified her role as a community leader by founding and chairing the Black Student Union, actively pushing for removing the Lee Statue and renaming the park formerly known as Lee Park. Her efforts culminated in success when 2017, the Charlottesville City Council voted to remove the statues.

Zyahna has maintained her dedication to fostering change, continually collaborating with local teens and youth leaders to address racial disparities, particularly in the education sector. Gradually, she emerged as a prominent figure within the BLM movement.

In January 2019, Zyahna showcased her literary talents by publishing “Reclaim,” a collection of poetry and essays.

Currently, she holds the distinction of being the youngest member of the inaugural Virginia African American Advisory Board, having been appointed by Governor Ralph Northam.

Her focus within this role remains on rectifying educational disparities and ensuring equitable access for youth of color. Currently, she is a Master’s student at the University of Virginia.
