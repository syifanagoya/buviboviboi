Storyline & Plot

Moon in the Day is a fantasy romance drama that tells the story of Do Ha and Han Ri Ta, two lovers from the Silla Dynasty who were separated by betrayal and death. Do Ha was a nobleman and a warrior who fought against the Gaya confederacy, a rival kingdom. He killed Ri Ta’s father, the general of Gaya, and executed her family.

Ri Ta, who was the sole survivor, swore to avenge her family and tried to kill Do Ha. However, she ended up falling in love with him, and they got engaged. On the day of their wedding, Ri Ta stabbed Do Ha in the chest, fulfilling her vow. Do Ha, who felt betrayed and heartbroken, cursed Ri Ta and vowed to kill her in her next life.

1500 years later, Do Ha’s spirit possessed the body of Han Joon Oh, a top star in Korea who was involved in a car accident. Kang Young Hwa, a firefighter who saved him from the accident, was the reincarnation of Ri Ta. She was hired to be his bodyguard, and they started to develop feelings for each other. However, Do Ha, who still remembered his past life, tried to kill Young Hwa, but failed due to a protective force that surrounded her.

He also realized that he still loved her, despite his anger and pain. Young Hwa, who had no memory of her past life, was confused by Do Ha’s actions and words, but also felt a strong connection to him. She also began to have dreams and visions of her past life, and the tragic events that led to Do Ha’s death.
