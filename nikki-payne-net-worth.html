FAQs About Nikki Payne

Who is Nikki Payne?

Nikki Payne, also known as Nikkie Payne, is a Canadian comedian and actress from Lower Sackville, Nova Scotia, Canada. She’s recognized for using her lisp, a result of a cleft palate, as part of her comedy act.

What are some key milestones in her early life?

She attended Nova Scotia Community College in Truro, where she acted as a sports team mascot. She also studied at Humber School of Comedy and The Second City Conservatory.

What awards has she won?

She has won three Canadian Comedy Awards: Best Stand-up Newcomer and Best Female Stand-up in 2003, 2005, and 2008.

In what shows has she appeared?

She has appeared in shows such as Last Comic Standing, Video on Trial, Buzz, Am I Right??, Satisfaction, and the NFB documentary The Next Big Thing. She’s also been featured in the Canadian sketch comedy show Comedy Inc.

What significant recognition did she receive in 2012?

In 2012, she was named Comedy Person of the Year at the annual Canadian Comedy Awards in Toronto.

Does she have any special projects related to nature?

Yes, in 2017, she hosted the Nature Conservancy of Canada’s documentary film, The Enlightened Redneck’s Guide to Moose Sex.

What personal challenges has she faced?

Apart from her cleft palate, she has dealt with partial facial paralysis due to Bell’s palsy and has donated a kidney to her father, an experience she also incorporates into her comedy.

How does she use her personal experiences in her comedy?

She often begins her comedy routines by acknowledging her lisp and uses her life’s challenges, including her medical experiences, to connect with and entertain her audience. Her ability to laugh at herself and life’s curveballs is a hallmark of her comedy.

What is her connection with the AboutFace organization?

She works with the AboutFace organization, which supports children with facial differences, sharing her experiences and using her platform to raise awareness.
