Early Life & Biography

Kondo has an interesting life since her childhood. In junior school days, Kondo ran into the classroom to tidy up bookshelves while her other classmates were playing in physical education classes. Whenever there were nominations for class roles, she did not seek to be the class representative. Instead, she continues to tidy up books. She has experienced a breakthrough in organizing one day. One day, she had a nervous breakdown and fainted. She was unconscious for two hours there. Then she realized that she should be doing is finding the things she wants to keep.

She spent five her years as an attendant maiden at a Shinto shrine. She also founded her organizing consulting business when she was at age 19 and a sociology student at Tokyo Woman’s Christian University. In her senior years, she wrote her capstone thesis titled “Tidying up as seen from the perspective of gender.”

Personal Life

Kondo gets married to Takumi Kawahara in 2012. When they both met for the first time, Kawahara was working in sales support and marketing at a corporation in Osaka. When Kondo’s career flew off, he left his job to become her manager and eventually and also CEO of Konmari Media, LLC. Then the couple later has two daughters together, Satsuki and Miko. After getting married, Kondo lived in Tokyo, and she later moved to San Francisco. Now in recent days, she and her family live in Los Angeles, California.
